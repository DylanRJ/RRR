The making of the music...
Within the roots culture there are some things we can instantly recognise as being indicative of the music and culture itself. The first of which is the large stacked soundsystems which are used to put on concerts or a 'dance'. The second being the two turntables, linked together by an audio mixer with capabilties of mixing tracks and inputting effects.
In this page we have interactive features where users can hover over the various images displayed and listen to the sounds of different sub-genres of roots music. Whilst also being presented with information about the particular sub-genre and the sound being played.
Stacks...
(Hover over the different levels of the stack to hear different genres of roots music and get some information about them)
Dub

Dub is a production-heavy roots reggae music often incorporating electronic technologies such as reverb and echo effects onto remixs of existing songs.
Sound: Scientist - "Curse of the Vampire"

Dancehall

Dancehall is a rhythm-heavy genre of roots music. It does not conform to the traditional structure of reggae music and drops the one-drop style and adopts a different time signature.
Sound: Vybz Kartel - "Bend Like Banana"

Mento

Mento is one of the earliest forms of roots music and encapsulates the spirit of roots music. A conscious appreciation of the position and situation of Jamaican peoples.
Sound: Harry Belafonte - "Banana Boat Song"


Mixer

The mixer is one of the fundamental parts of roots music, adding the effects and mixing the tracks which appear on each turntable.
Decks...
The second significant and instantly recognisable thing we can see is the two turntables and mixer which used in order to play and mix tracks together. This is an essential part of performing live music and creating the comixture of sounds which encompass a roots performance. Many audio effects are achieved through the utilisation of the mixer and record synchronisation in tandem to create sound effects. (Hover over the turntables to hear sounds effects which asre typical of roots and dub culture. These Sound Effects include Reverb, Echo, Phaser, Tape Delay, Harmonisation and Scratch.)
